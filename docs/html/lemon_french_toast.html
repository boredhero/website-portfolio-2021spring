Cooking with Noah: Lemon French Toast

Lemon French Toast

Ingredients

  • Large Flat Skillet or Griddle (Nonstick or cast-iron preferred)
  • A good spatula
  • Whisk (Optional but I prefer it)
  • Metal Tongs
  • Butter
  • 1 Fresh Lemon
  • 1-2 Eggs
  • 1/4-1/2 cup milk or cream
  • 1/4c Sugar (Optional depending on whether you want the bread to be sweet or you want it to be tart and compensate with sweet topings like syrup)
  • Bread Slices (A thick cut Brioche is my fav but any will work generally)
  • 1/2 tsp lemon extract (Optional)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (Optional)
  • Fresh Raspberries or other fresh berries (Optional Garnish)
  • Maple Syrup (Optional Topping)
  • Powdered Sugar (Optional Topping)
  • Chocolate Sauce (Optional Topping)

Procedure

If making this for one, use 1 egg, 1/4 cup milk/cream, and 1/8 cup of lemon juice, otherwise use the higher range from the ingredients list
  1. Zest your lemon and add zest to a large mixing bowl
  2. Juice your lemon. I juice and then measure because too much lemon juice will affect texture. 1/8c to 1/4c is the range you're looking for for this. Add juice to bowl
  3. Add any lemon or vanilla extract you may optionally be using to the bowl and whisk.
  4. If you want your toast to be sweet, add the optional 1/4 cup sugar here and whisk until dissolved. If not, skip this step.
  5. Add your egg and milk to the mixture and whisk rapidly to fully combine/emulsify the mixture
  6. Add a pat of butter to your skillet on low-med heat (I prefer to use lower heat for longer times with french toast to avoid a raw center, but with a bit of skill higher temperatures can be used carefully to avoid burning)
  7. Dip your slices of bread into the mixture and make sure they fully absorb it, then add to the skillet and cover with a lid to allow steam to work on it without burning it.
  8. In a few minutes, peek under the edge with your spatula. You want a nice golden-brown texture. At this point, you can flip them and uncover for the rest of the cook.
  9. Once both sides are browned, you can serve. I like to plate them with fresh raspberries and maple or chocolate syrup, and an additional pat of butter on top.
